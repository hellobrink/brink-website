Pritika is an Innovation Manager, her work blends systemic approaches to innovation to coaching ventures. She’s currently working on the Better Futures CoLab, leading the Oxygen CoLab’s ‘scaling commercially’ portfolio; delivering Enabel Innovation Hub’s portfolio of 21 ventures seeking to transition-to-scale and exploring ways to improve livelihoods across informality. She has just returned from two and a half years in Nairobi, Kenya where she was part of the team setting up Brink East Africa. Pritika has over 8 years of experience across innovation, research, climate change and international development. Before Brink, Pritika worked in International Climate Finance for the UK Government, working with decision-makers and Governments on the UK’s diplomatic relationship with Colombia and a portfolio of climate projects, as well as managing two climate investment vehicles operating predominantly in Africa. She has a MSC in Global Prosperity, exploring transformative entrepreneurship, innovation theory and systems change perspectives - exploring in depth how new technologies and governance mechanisms such as blockchain can enable alternative economies. She has subsequently contributed to research paper on Universal Basic Services a growing policy movement in the UK.

Pritika’s passion for venturing started in 2015 when she started a social enterprise called Vestability, which produced a circular economy solution for sustainable, low-cost life jackets to reduce drowning deaths in East Africa. Bumbling through lean start-up and human centred design approaches (and in hindsight doing everything in the wrong order), she learnt first hand all the challenges of setting up a venture within a development context, co-designing products with users and testing out MVPs in Madagascar with two fishing villages and learning the resilience required to champion an idea.
